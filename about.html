Humanity finds itself facing an uncertain period. A long-overdue rise in developing world prosperity is being accompanied by a future of serious environmental challenges and volatile economic growth. Global populations are growing, focusing mainly in cities. If we are to find a path to low-carbon, prosperous, climate-neutral future, we must do so in our urban areas.

Urban Metabolism

Cities have long been envisaged as national/global economic engines, being supported through the resouces drawn from hinterlands. Fuel, food, construction materials, and water constantly flow into cities, transformed by the processes that provide us with nurishment, comfort, employment, and entertainment. After their use, these resources exit our cities in a multitude of "waste" streams - exhaust fumes, smokestack plumes, garbage, sewage, and low-grade heat. But research shows us that the values in these waste streams hasn't fully been exploited; how can we improve the efficiency of these transformative processes, as well as extract the valuable resources from these waste flows to inch closer to sustainable cities?

Cities - The Problem and the Solution

In order for cities to be the future of our economic success, they need to cease to be a source our environmental problems. We need to find ways to use energy much more efficiently in our buildings and transportation networks. We need to reduce the seemingly insatiable demand that is placed on our natural support systems. We need to work towards closing the nutrient, hydrological, and material loops, rather than continue their linear flow through our systems that transform them into "waste". With the leaps in inginuity that cities enable, this is all within our grasp. My research investigates problems within this realm. I invite you to examine some of the answers I've found to this point.
